INSTALLING AND USING YOUR BIBLE STUDY APP
==========================================

This app is a website, not something from an app store. Once it is hosted
somewhere (GitHub Pages, Netlify, or even just run from your own PC -- see
the PC section below), you visit it in your browser and add it to your
home screen. No install step, no app-store account needed.

If you have not hosted it anywhere yet, the fastest free option is GitHub
Pages: push this folder to a GitHub repo and enable Pages for it in the
repo's Settings. That gives you a web address like:

    https://yourname.github.io/BibleStudy/

Use that address in place of "the app's URL" below.


1. IPHONE (SAFARI)
-------------------
1. Open the app's URL in Safari (it must be Safari, not Chrome, for this
   step to work on iOS).
2. Tap the Share button (the square with an arrow pointing up) in the
   bottom toolbar.
3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen".
4. Give it a name if you like, then tap "Add".
5. The app now has an icon on your home screen and opens full-screen,
   like a regular app.

To remove it: press and hold the icon, then choose "Remove App" /
"Delete". This does not delete anything from the server -- reinstalling
just re-adds the shortcut.

Offline use: the first time you open the app, it saves itself and the
core Bible text for offline reading. Open Settings (the icon in the top
right) and turn on "Download entire Bible for offline use" while on
Wi-Fi, so every book's Greek/Hebrew interlinear works without a
connection.


2. ANDROID (CHROME)
--------------------
1. Open the app's URL in Chrome.
2. Tap the three-dot menu (top right).
3. Tap "Add to Home screen". On some versions of Chrome this appears
   automatically as an "Install app" banner or an icon in the address
   bar -- tap that instead if you see it.
4. Confirm the name and tap "Add" / "Install".
5. The app icon appears on your home screen and app drawer, and opens in
   its own window without Chrome's address bar.

To uninstall: press and hold the icon and choose "Uninstall", the same
as any other Android app.

Offline use: same as iPhone -- open Settings and use "Download entire
Bible for offline use" on Wi-Fi. Turn off "Only download data on Wi-Fi"
in Settings if you are fine using cellular data for downloads instead.


3. PC (WINDOWS / MAC / LINUX)
------------------------------
You have two options.

Option A -- Use the hosted version (recommended):
1. Open the app's URL in Chrome or Edge.
2. Click the install icon in the address bar (a small monitor-with-arrow
   icon on the right side of the address bar), or open the browser's
   menu and choose "Install this site as an app".
3. It opens in its own window from then on, with a shortcut in your
   Start Menu, Applications folder, or Dock, like a native app.

Option B -- Run it directly from this folder (no hosting needed):
1. Double-click index.html in this folder to open it in your browser.
   This works right away for reading, searching, and the Greek/Hebrew
   interlinear.
2. The "install as an app" step and full offline caching need the app
   to be served over a real web address (http/https), not opened as a
   plain file. For those, run a small local server instead:
     - Open a command prompt / terminal in this folder.
     - Run:  python -m http.server 8000
     - Then visit http://localhost:8000 in your browser.
3. From http://localhost:8000 you can install it as an app (see Option
   A, step 2) and use the offline-download button in Settings.


NOTES FOR ALL PLATFORMS
------------------------
- Bible versions: pick any combination of ASV, KJV, and YLT in Settings
  -- each active version displays in parallel, verse by verse.

- Greek/Hebrew interlinear: turn on separately for the New Testament
  (Greek) and Old Testament (Hebrew) in Settings. Tap any original-
  language word for its lexicon definition and grammar explanation.

- Commentary: turn on Matthew Henry (1710) and/or Jamieson-Fausset-Brown
  (1871) in Settings. A small horn icon appears next to verses that have
  a note from an active commentary -- tap it to read.

- Notes: hover or tap a verse, chapter title, or book title (look for
  the feather-pen cursor) and double-click / double-tap to add a
  personal note. Manage notes -- edit, delete, or move to a different
  reference -- from the panel that opens.

- Search: tap the magnifying-glass icon. It automatically detects Bible
  references (like "John 3:16"), English words and phrases, Greek or
  Hebrew words and phrases, and Strong's numbers (like "G2316").

- Maps: tap the map icon for a gallery of historical maps of the
  biblical world, from the time of the patriarchs through the early
  church.

- Archaeological Finds: tap the lightning-bolt icon for a different real
  archaeological discovery each day, with a photo, a short description,
  a link to its Wikipedia article, and the related Bible passages.

- Everything you create (notes, settings, downloaded data) is stored
  only on your own device -- nothing is sent to a server, except for the
  optional live fetches of extra English translations and the linked
  Wikimedia photos and map images.
